The winch system is composed of a simple and compound gear train. Both are powered by an FT Motor and a 7.96 V power source. The systemâ€™s movement can be powered on and off through the use of an FT switch. First, the small gear in the FT motor is meshed to a red FT gear, creating a simple gear train. This red gear is put onto an axle which it shares with the winch drum. The red, circular plates on the sides of the drum had holes going around the circumference and therefore our group decided to put short poles through these holes to create a cylinder shape, mimicking a larger drum. Tied onto one of these poles is a double-strand piece of string -- portraying a cord in an actual winch -- attached to a 180-gram weight which is reeled in as the winch rotates. To theoretically test the measurements we used, NetLogo programming to determine the voltage, current, and the mass, if we increased or decreased drum size.
